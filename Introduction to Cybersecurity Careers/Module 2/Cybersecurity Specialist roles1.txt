Cybersecurity Specialist:

Similar Jobs: IT specialists, Cybersecurity specialists, Information security specialists, IT security specialists, and IT information security specialists
A Cybersecurity Specialist’s job is to maintain the security of an organization’s IT infrastructure. They scrutinize systems for impending threats, examine and evaluate security breaches, and apply appropriate security measures to prevent future cyberattacks. The role also involves continuous learning and keeping up to date with the latest and evolving cybersecurity threats.

Cybercrime Analyst:
Similar Jobs: Digital forensics analysts, Cyber forensic analysts, Digital forensic examiners and Security investigators

A Cybercrime Analyst is responsible for recognizing and exploring patterns and trends in cybercrime activities. Their role involves foreseeing potential future attacks based on historical data and current trends. They provide support in developing sustainable defense strategies. When a breach occurs, they perform digital forensics investigations, collect evidence and report relevant findings to stakeholders, including law enforcement agencies, as required.


Intrusion and Incident Analyst:

Similar Jobs: Cybersecurity incident response analysts, Incident response analysts, Incident response specialists and Information security analysts

An Intrusion and Incident Analyst is responsible for identifying and responding to cybersecurity breaches, locating the source, and examining the extent of the damage. They develop and employ containment strategies to lessen the impact, perform thorough investigations to comprehend the intrusion’s cause, and participate in recovery efforts after the incident. They work with the cybersecurity team to improve protective strategies, blocking future intrusions.


IT Auditor:

Similar Jobs: IT audit managers, IT internal auditors, IT service delivery managers, Audit and assurance managers


An IT Auditor’s role is to investigate and examine an organization’s IT systems, practices, and operations to ensure compliance with laws and industry standards. They recognize potential security risks and breaches, advise necessary changes to strengthen IT security, and conduct regular audits to validate the effectiveness of security measures and protocols. They aim to protect the organization’s data from unauthorized access or alterations.
